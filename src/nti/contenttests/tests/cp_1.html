Challenge Problems

  1. The squares of two consecutive positive integers differ by 67. What is the smaller of the two integers?  

    Hint: How do you get from one perfect square to the next quickly?

  2. The Indian mathematician Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920) knew that there are four different positive integers A, B, C, and D such that A3+B3=1729 and C3+D3=1729 . What is the sum A+B+C+D ? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  3. Express 25⋅83⋅162 as a power of 4.

  4. What five-digit positive integer with an 8 in the ten-thousands place is the cube of an integer?

  5. Express 316 as a power of 19 .  

    Hint: Can you write it as a power of 9?

  6. Express 22×42×82×162×…×10242 as a power of 2. (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  7. When the expression 810⋅522 is multiplied out, how many digits does the number have? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)  , 

    Hint: Zeros at the end of a number---what number does that make you think about multiplying by? Hint: How can you get 10's in the product?

  8. For what value of n does 500,0002⋅200,0002=10n ?

  9. Find the number n such that n⋅34⋅25=66 .

  10. What is the sum of the digits of the number 22005⋅52007⋅3 ? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  11. What is the positive integer N for which 222⋅552=102⋅N2 ? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  12. Let a and b be numbers. Simplify the following expressions. Express each of your answers as a number times a power of a times a power of b.

    2

    1. (2ab2)3

    2. 5a2b(2ab)3

  13. Let a, b, and c be numbers. Simplify the following expressions. In each of your answers, the variables a, b, and c should each appear only once.

    2

    1. a2b⋅8ab6c2

    2. (a2)4(ab)3c3

  14. What is the largest integer n for which n2000 is less than 53000 ? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)  

  15. For what value of x is 125⋅55=5x+5x+5x+5x+5x ?

  16. What is the value of x in the equation (2x)(303)=(23)(33)(43)(53) ?
    (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  17. Let a and b be numbers.

    1. Show that (a+b)2=a2+2ab+b2 .

    2. Show that (a−b)2=a2−2ab+b2 .

    3. Show that (a+b)3=a3+3a2b+3ab2+b3 .

  18. Let a and b be numbers.

    1. Show that (a−b)(a+b)=a2−b2 .

    2. Show that (a−b)(a2+ab+b2)=a3−b3 .  

      Hint: The distributive property works with sums of three numbers just like it does with sums of two numbers: w(x+y+z)=wx+wy+wz .

    3. Show that (a+b)(a2−ab+b2)=a3+b3 .

  19. What integer n has the property that 596 is greater than n72 and 596 is less than (n+1)72 ? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)  

    Hint: Can the numbers in the problem be written with the same exponent?

  20. The perfect squares from 1 through 1225 are printed as a sequence of digits

    1491625…1225.

    How many digits are in the sequence? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)
